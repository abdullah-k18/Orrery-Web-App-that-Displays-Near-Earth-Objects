Itokawa

Itokawa is a near-Earth asteroid named after the Japanese engineer Hideo Itokawa. It measures about 0.33 kilometers (0.20 miles) in length and is classified as an S-type asteroid. Itokawa is significant for being the first asteroid visited by a spacecraft, the Hayabusa mission, which collected samples from its surface and returned them to Earth in 2010. The asteroid's irregular shape and surface features provide valuable insights into the composition and history of asteroids in the solar system.
