Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, beyond Neptune. It was discovered on February 18, 1930, by Clyde Tombaugh. Pluto has a diameter of about 2,377 kilometers (1,477 miles) and is known for its heart-shaped glacier, known as Tombaugh Regio. It takes approximately 248 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun. In 2006, Pluto was reclassified from a planet to a dwarf planet by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).
