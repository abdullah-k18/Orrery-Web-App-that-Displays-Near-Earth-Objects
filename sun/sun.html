Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System and is the most important source of energy for life on Earth. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, with temperatures reaching about 15 million degrees Celsius (27 million degrees Fahrenheit) at its core. The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%). It has a diameter of approximately 1.39 million kilometers (864,000 miles) and is located about 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) away from Earth. The Sun plays a crucial role in the gravitational dynamics of the Solar System.
