Eros

Eros is an asteroid and one of the largest near-Earth asteroids. Discovered in 1898, it has an irregular shape and is approximately 16.84 kilometers (10.45 miles) long. Eros is classified as a S-type asteroid, primarily composed of silicate materials and metallic nickel-iron. It has been visited by spacecraft, including NASA's NEAR Shoemaker mission, which orbited and landed on Eros in 2001. This asteroid poses a potential impact risk to Earth but also provides valuable insights into the formation and evolution of our solar system.
